Six concepts. One page. Use this before you invest a single pound.

01 · The two questions
Question one
If something went wrong tomorrow — could you handle it without selling your investments?
You need: yes
Question two
Is anything you owe costing you more than the market is likely to give you back?
You need: no
Yes to question one. No to question two. That is ready. Not optimised — protected.
02 · The three layers
1
Safety net — cash, accessible, not invested
Three to six months of essential expenses. This is not an investment. It is the buffer that stops one bad month from forcing you to sell at the wrong time. Until it exists — nothing moves to the market.
2
Goals — medium term, specific target
A real goal with a real date. A house deposit. A career pause. A move. The investment choice depends on how far away the date is — closer goals need less risk, not more.
3
Growth — long term, where the market works
Money you will not touch for ten years or more. This is where compound growth does its job. The longer the runway, the more volatility becomes noise rather than risk.
